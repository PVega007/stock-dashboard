Stock Analysis Prompt Framework
Use this structure to stress-test any investment candidate. Treat any screening grade or article recommendation as a hypothesis to test — not a conclusion to validate.
Supplementary Prompts
Key Principles
Screening grades are a starting point, never a conclusion. GuruFocus and similar tools cannot detect governance issues, recent legal proceedings, or management credibility problems.
A stock down 30%+ in 90 days always has a specific reason. Find it before doing anything else — it may change everything that follows.
For cyclicals: always model trough earnings. A 10x P/E on peak earnings can be a 50x P/E on trough earnings.
Management credibility is non-negotiable. Guidance cuts + coinciding accounting benefits = require large additional margin of safety.
📐 Entry Target Methodology
v3 · June 2026
Formula
Forward EPS  = Current Price ÷ Forward P/E (consensus)
Tier 1 floor   = Forward EPS × Low P/E × (1 − MoS)   ← most conservative entry
Tier 1 ceiling = Forward EPS × Low P/E                     ← low-end fair value
Tier 2 ceiling = Forward EPS × High P/E × (1 − ½ MoS) ← bull-case fair value; half MoS discount
Bear Floor    = T1 floor × (1 − bear discount)          ← recession / crash scenario floor
Execution Confirmation Layer (v3 New)
Before deploying capital when price reaches T1 zone, check 2+ of 4 signals:
  1. RSI (14-day) < 45 — confirms oversold condition aligned with entry
  2. MACD daily converging — histogram bars shrinking or bullish cross printed
  3. Price at/below 50-day SMA — aligns entry with institutional support
  4. Price below 9-day EMA — confirms stock in drawdown, not a dip on uptrend

2+ signals in T1 zone → HIGH CONVICTION — scale up tranche size
0–1 signal in T1 zone → deploy minimum tranche only, wait for confirmation

P/E Range Philosophy
Tight range (e.g. MSFT 28–34×) → high confidence in valuation band → entry target is reliable
Wide range (e.g. LLY 25–50×) → genuine uncertainty about pipeline success → band is the honest signal
Non-P/E metric (e.g. CRWV 12–18× EV/Rev) → pre-profitability; model communicates range of outcomes rather than false precision
Low range (e.g. HPQ 8–11×) → declining market or commodity business — premium is not warranted
Special Case Handling
MU — Cyclical: uses mid-cycle normalized EPS (~$50/share), NOT peak forward EPS ($102.99). Apply 22–28× to normalized figure only.
FRVO — Pre-revenue geothermal: uses EV/PPA contracted backlog (1.5–2.0×) on per-share backlog value ($26.77). No P/E until first commercial revenue.
HESM — MLP: uses P/DCF (11–14×) on distributable cash flow per unit (~$2.90/unit). Not GAAP EPS.
IAU / REMX — Hard assets: use price-based support levels (historical 52-wk lows, 200-day SMA). No traditional P/E.
ETFs — No per-share EPS in FMP: use blended forward P/E from fund provider data (see per-stock table above for agreed ranges). Recalibrate annually.
🚨 Reassessment Triggers
These events require reopening the model — not just checking price vs. target.
Single-Stock — Reassess That Position Immediately
• Earnings miss or beat >15% vs. consensus → forward EPS has changed; recalculate
• Management guidance cut >10% on revenue or EPS
• CEO / CFO departure or major insider selling event
• New SEC investigation, class action, or regulatory proceeding filed
• Single-day price move >20% (up or down) — find the specific cause first
• Customer concentration event: top customer loss or major contract win/loss
Sector-Wide — Reassess All Stocks in That Category
• Semiconductor inventory correction signal: book-to-bill ratio falls below 0.9 for 2+ consecutive months
• New major trade restriction, tariff, or export control affecting the sector
• Hyperscaler capex guidance cut >10% (primary signal for NVDA, MRVL, AMAT, TER)
• Disruptive technology shift (e.g. new chip architecture, GLP-1 competitor approval for pharma)
• Sector ETF drawdown >20% from recent high in <60 days
Portfolio-Wide — Reassess MoS Tiers & P/E Ranges for All Holdings
• Fed rate change of ±50bps or more in a single meeting (discount rate shift affects all multiples)
• S&P 500 correction >10% in <30 days — re-examine whether P/E ranges still reflect reality
• VIX sustained above 30 for >2 weeks — risk premium has shifted; MoS tiers may need upward revision
• Recession probability >60% (Moody's / NY Fed model) — cycle-top P/E ranges must compress
• 10-year Treasury yield moves >75bps in either direction in 60 days — duration stocks re-price
• Annual calendar review: reassess all forward EPS inputs each January and July as fiscal years roll
